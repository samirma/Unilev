Eswap Protocol: Shariah Compliance Executive Case

A Definitive Technical & Legal Justification

To construct the "maximum proof" Al-Adillah al-Qat'iyyah for the Shariah compliance of Eswap, we must align the protocol’s technical architecture directly with the primary sources of Islamic Law (Qur'an & Sunnah) and the established Fiqh Maxims.

1. The Primary Pillar: The "Aydan bi-Ayd" (Hand-to-Hand) Requirement

The Proof (Dalil): The Prophet (ﷺ) said: الذهب بالذهب والفضة بالفضة... مثلا بمثل يدا بيد... فإذا اختلفت هذه الأصناف فبيعوا كيف شئتم إذا كان يدا بيد "Gold for gold, silver for silver... like for like, hand to hand. If these classes differ, then sell as you wish, provided it is hand to hand." (Sahih Muslim).
The Technical Adillah: In conventional margin, "possession" is often a delay or a ledger entry. In Eswap, the exchange happens via Uniswap V3 in a single Atomic Transaction.
The Verdict: Because the swap occurs at the exact moment the position is opened, Taqabud (Immediate Possession) is achieved. The "Hand-to-Hand" requirement is fulfilled digitally through the blockchain’s instant finality.

2. The Absolute Prohibition of Riba (Interest)

The Proof (Dalil): وَأَحَلَّ اللَّهُ الْبَيْعَ وَحَرَّمَ الرِّبَا "But Allah has permitted trade and has forbidden interest (Riba)." (Qur’an 2:275).
The Technical Adillah: Every traditional leverage protocol (Aave, Compound, dYdX) uses an interest-rate model where a "Borrower" pays a "Lender" a percentage for the duration of the loan.
The Verdict: Eswap operates with a 0% Interest Rate. Whether the position is held for a day or a decade, the user owes exactly the principal amount back to the pool. This eliminates Riba al-Nasi'ah (Interest from Delay) entirely.

3. The Validation of "Ujrah" (Service Fee) over "Qard" (Loan)

The Proof (Dalil): The Fiqh Maxim states: كل قرض جر نفعاً فهو ربا "Every loan that attracts a benefit is Riba." (Kullu qardin jarra naf'an fahuwa riba).
The Technical Adillah: Eswap does not profit from the act of lending. Instead, it charges a Trade Fee at the point of execution. This is categorized as Ujrah (Payment for Labor/Service).
The Verdict: The protocol is a service provider, not a money-lender. Charging a fee for the service of facilitating a trade is Halal under the rules of Ijarah.

4. Verification of Ownership (Al-Milkiyah) via NFT

The Proof (Dalil): The Prophet (ﷺ) forbade selling what you do not own (Bay' ma la yamlika).
The Technical Adillah: Eswap represents every position as a unique NFT (ERC721). In modern Islamic finance, this is called Sard al-Milkiyah (Digital Title of Ownership).
The Verdict: The trader is the owner of an NFT that has a direct claim on the underlying assets. Since the user "owns" the NFT, they have the legal right to close the position for profit (Bay’ al-Mulkiyya).

5. Elimination of Gharar (Uncertainty) via Oracle Transparency

The Proof (Dalil): The Prophet (ﷺ) forbade the sale of items of excessive uncertainty (Gharar).
The Technical Adillah: Uncertainty in trading often comes from hidden prices. Eswap uses Chainlink Oracles, which provide decentralized, transparent, and public prices.
The Verdict: Every detail is Ma'lum (Known) before the transaction. There is zero ambiguity in the contract, which removes the risk of Gharar al-Fahish.

6. The Structure of "Musharakah" (Risk Sharing)

The Proof (Dalil): الغنم بالغرم "Profit follows risk." (Al-ghunmu bi al-ghurmi).
The Technical Adillah: Liquidity Providers do not receive a guaranteed return. Their yield depends on the volume of trades. They take risk in exchange for a share of the protocol's success.
The Verdict: This aligns with the Musharakah (Partnership) model. The LPs are partners, and their reward is tied to actual economic activity.

7. Conclusion

Eswap is a Halal alternative to margin trading because it transforms the transaction from a Debt-Based Relationship into a Trade-Based Relationship.

  1. Possession is Immediate (Atomic Swaps).
  2. Interest is Zero (No Riba).
  3. Ownership is Certified (NFTs).
  4. Risk is Shared (LP/Musharakah).
  5. Terms are Transparent (Oracles).
